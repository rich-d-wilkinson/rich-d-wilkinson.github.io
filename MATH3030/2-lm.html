In the standard linear model, the response variable, \(y\), is univariate and the mean of \(y\), \(\mu={\mathbb{E}}[y]\), is modelled as a linear function of the elements of a covariate vector \(\mathbf x=(x_1, \ldots , x_p)^\top \in \mathbb{R}^p\), i.e.Â it is assumed that \[ \mu = \boldsymbol \beta^\top \mathbf x, \] where \(\boldsymbol \beta\in \mathbb{R}^p\) is an unknown parameter vector to be estimated. In this chapter we consider a generalisation of the standard linear model in which the response, \(\mathbf y\), is now a \(q \times 1\) vector. In this setting, the linear model takes the form \[ {\pmb \mu}=\mathbf B^\top \mathbf x, \] where the mean vector \({\boldsymbol{\mu}}\) is \(q \times 1\), the covariate vector \(\mathbf x\) is \(p \times 1\) and the parameter vector \(\mathbf B\) is \(p \times q\). The reason for having \(\mathbf B^\top\) above rather than simply \(\mathbf B\) will become clear later in the chapter.
